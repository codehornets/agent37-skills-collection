For years, we've heard two major narratives about AI. One predicting the end of human work, the other dismissing it as
hype. The truth is more nuanced, and more hopeful.

From radiology to software engineering, the pattern repeats: as technology makes tasks cheaper and faster, demand for
human creativity and judgment grows.

Garry explores what history, economics, and real companies show us— that technology doesn't replace people, it redefines
what we can do.
